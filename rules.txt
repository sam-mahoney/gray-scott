
==== rules ====

∂U/∂t = Du * ∇²U - U*V² + F*(1 - U)

∂V/∂t = Dv * ∇²V + U*V² - (F + k)*V

==== meaning ====

U: The Substrate 
-- spreads to neighboring cells (diffusion at rate `Du`)
-- consumed where it meets V (reaction)
-- replenished toward concentration 1 from outside (the "feed" at rate `F`)

V: The Autocatalyst
-- spreads to neighboring cells (diffusion at rate `Dv`)
-- additional V is created where U and V meet (V consumes U -> produces more V)
-- is removed (the "kill" at rate `F+k`)

==== understanding ====
(a) why does V grow in regions where U is high, (b) but stop growing where U is depleted?

a: V takes U as input. It then outputs additional V. Seed of V is required to start reaction.
b: V is removed quicker than it can diffuse to areas of high U. V is constantly
dying. V requires U to grow.
